The product

What COLAClear is

COLAClear pre-screens wine, spirits, and beer labels against U.S. federal and state regulations (27 CFR Parts 4, 5, 7, and 16) before a producer submits them to the TTB for label approval. Upload a label, get a color-coded compliance report in seconds. It's a real, live SaaS product with paying-category customers — not a demo.

The challenge

A brand-new product in a niche category has no authority and no traffic. Worse, its buyers — small wineries, craft distillers, importers — don't search in industry jargon. They search in the language of a problem: "why did TTB reject my label," "how to fix a rejected COLA." And increasingly, they don't search at all — they ask an AI. To win, COLAClear had to be found on Google and be the tool an AI names when asked.

The bar to clear: when a producer asks ChatGPT or Google's AI "is there a tool that checks my label before I file with the TTB?" — the answer should be COLAClear, described correctly.

What was done — the playbook, applied

  • Real-intent keywords. Strategy built on SEMrush, SpyFu, and Search Console around commercial-intent phrases like "COLA prescreen tool" and "how to fix a rejected COLA label."
  • Structured data. schema.org markup — Organization, SoftwareApplication, Person, Review — so machines can restate what the tool does, accurately.
  • An llms.txt brief. A root-level file written for AI systems, stating scope and the exact regulations checked.
  • AI-crawler access. robots and crawler configuration that lets answer engines read and quote the site.
  • Question-shaped pages. A 16-pair FAQ and a methodology page mapped to how producers actually ask.
  • Internal linking. FAQ, articles, and the tool deliberately woven together.
  • A content engine. Ongoing industry analyses plus trade-press bylines in Wine Business Monthly, Wine Industry Advisor, and Craft Spirits Magazine.

The result

Within months of launch, and with zero paid media, Google's AI Overview cites COLAClear by name as the automated TTB pre-screening tool — reproducing its compliance logic and its pass / review / fail output. Alongside it, the site ranks page-one for the high-intent searches its buyers actually run, and the trade-press bylines rank on those same searches, reinforcing the signal.

Why it matters: this is GEO working exactly as intended. The AI didn't just link to COLAClear — it named it and explained it, turning a generic question into a specific recommendation. That's the difference between being on the internet and being the answer.

This is repeatable
