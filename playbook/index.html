The Playbook

Eight mechanics of being found

Discoverability isn't luck or a secret. It's eight things done deliberately — so search engines rank you and AI assistants cite you by name. Each one below is shown with a real example from COLAClear.

SEO

Search engines

Google and Bing crawl and rank pages. The goal: show up on page one for the searches your buyers actually run.

AEO

Answer engines

Featured snippets and "People Also Ask." The goal: be the answer at the top, not just a blue link below it.

GEO

Generative engines

ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Google's AI Overviews synthesize an answer and cite sources. The goal: be the source they name.

It used to be "get to page one." Now it's "be the answer the machine gives." Same discipline, new surface.

1

Chase real intent, not vanity keywords

Rank for what buyers actually type when they're ready to act — not the biggest, vaguest term. Built on real search data (SEMrush, SpyFu, Google Search Console).

COLAClear: targets "COLA prescreen tool" and "how to fix a rejected COLA label" — high-intent phrases a producer types mid-problem — instead of the hopeless, generic "wine labels."

2

One name, everywhere

Pick one name and one address and use them identically across your site, profiles, and citations. Split names split your authority into weak signals instead of one strong one.

The cautionary tale: a company appearing three ways — Tory, GetTory.co, GetTory.AI — teaches machines it's three faint things, not one credible one. Consolidating to a single identity is often the highest-leverage fix.

3

Structured data (schema.org)

Machine-readable tags in your page's code that tell engines exactly what you are — an organization, a product, a person, a review — instead of making them guess.

COLAClear: ships Organization, SoftwareApplication, Person, and Review markup, so an AI can restate what the tool does, who built it, and that it's well reviewed — accurately.

4

An llms.txt brief for the AIs

A plain-text file at your site root written for AI systems — a concise brief on who you are, what you do, and the facts you want restated correctly.

COLAClear: publishes an llms.txt summarizing its scope and the regulations it checks — the same facts that later showed up, correctly, in Google's AI Overview.

5

Let the answer engines in

Your robots.txt and crawler settings decide which bots may read your site. Block the AI crawlers and you can't be cited; the invitation has to be explicit.

COLAClear: configures robots and AI crawlers deliberately, so the systems that build AI answers are allowed to read and quote it.

6

Question-shaped pages

Structure content the way people (and AIs) ask — real questions with direct answers. A good FAQ and a clear "how it works" page are AI citation magnets.

COLAClear: a 16-pair FAQ and a methodology page map to the exact questions producers ask — and hand answer engines clean, quotable passages.

7

Internal linking

Connect your pages to each other on purpose — FAQ to article to product — so both readers and crawlers can follow the thread, and authority flows between them.

COLAClear: deliberately links FAQ, articles, and the tool together, so a visitor (or crawler) who lands anywhere is guided to the rest.

8

The content engine

Authority is earned, not declared. Publish genuinely useful, data-driven material on a regular cadence — and place bylines in the press your audience already reads.

COLAClear: ongoing industry analyses plus bylines in Wine Business Monthly, Wine Industry Advisor, and Craft Spirits Magazine — the content engine behind the rankings.
